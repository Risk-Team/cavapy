# cavapy Logo Design

**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Status:** Implemented

## Decision Summary

Replaced the original mixed-element logo (grid panel, partial arc, contour lines, bar chart) with a focused, cohesive identity.

## Design

**Icon:** Hexagonal monogram badge with a teal-to-ocean gradient (`#2ca58d` → `#1d6f8c`), containing a white `c` in IBM Plex Mono. A subtle inner ring adds depth.

**Wordmark:** `c` in teal (`#2ca58d`) followed by `avapy` in deep navy (`#0b2239`), IBM Plex Sans, 68px, weight 700. The leading `c` echoes the badge color, linking icon to name.

**Separator:** Hairline in `#d0e4ef` between wordmark and subtitle.

**Subtitle:** "Climate data access for CAVA workflows" in muted steel blue (`#4a7a96`), IBM Plex Sans, 19px.

**Canvas:** `760 × 170` viewBox, no background fill (transparent — works on any background).

## File

`figures/cavapy_logo.svg`

## Rationale

- The old logo mixed unrelated scientific elements with no clear hierarchy.
- The hex monogram is compact, works at small sizes, and is recognizable as a software package mark.
- Teal/ocean palette is retained for brand continuity with the CAVA ecosystem.
- The `c` color split in the wordmark creates visual cohesion between badge and text without adding noise.
